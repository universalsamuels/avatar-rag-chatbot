Aang

Aang is the Avatar and the last surviving Airbender, born into the Air Nomads. He is the protagonist of Avatar: The Last Airbender. Aang was discovered by Monk Gyatso to be the Avatar at a young age, but the news that he was the Avatar was kept from him for some time out of concern from the Air Nomad elders, who worried about the pressure it would place on him.

Overwhelmed by the responsibility of being the Avatar, a young Aang ran away from the Southern Air Temple during a storm. He and his flying bison, Appa, were caught in the storm and crashed into the ocean, where Aang unconsciously created an iceberg air bubble that preserved his life in suspended animation for one hundred years.

During this century, the Fire Nation launched a genocidal campaign against the Air Nomads, wiping out the Air Nomad civilization because the Fire Lord feared the new Avatar would be an Airbender who could stop the Fire Nation's plans for conquest. Aang awoke from the iceberg to find Katara and Sokka of the Southern Water Tribe, who became his close friends and traveling companions.

As the Avatar, Aang is responsible for maintaining balance between the four nations: the Water Tribes, Earth Kingdom, Fire Nation, and Air Nomads. He must master all four bending elements (airbending, waterbending, earthbending, and firebending) in the Avatar State in order to defeat Fire Lord Ozai and end the Hundred Year War.

Aang is known for his airbending mastery, his pacifist values rooted in Air Nomad philosophy, and his refusal to take a life even of his enemies. This created significant internal conflict, as many believed the only way to stop Ozai was to kill him. Aang ultimately found an alternative: he used energybending, a rare and dangerous technique taught to him by the Lion Turtle, to remove Ozai's bending ability entirely rather than killing him.

Aang's animal guide and closest companion is Appa, his flying bison. He also has a pet lemur named Momo. Aang's romantic interest throughout the series is Katara, and the two are confirmed to marry and have children, including Tenzin, who appears prominently in The Legend of Korra as an Airbending master and political figure.

After the war, Aang worked to rebuild the Air Nomad culture and founded new Air Temples and an Air Acolyte movement of people dedicated to preserving Air Nomad teachings. He also helped found Republic City alongside Fire Lord Zuko as a place where people from all four nations could live together. Aang passed away of natural causes before the events of The Legend of Korra, after which Korra became his Avatar successor.
