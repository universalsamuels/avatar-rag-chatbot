Zuko

Zuko is the crown prince of the Fire Nation, son of Fire Lord Ozai and Ursa, and older brother of Azula. Zuko begins the original series as one of the show's central antagonists, obsessively pursuing the Avatar in order to restore his honor and reclaim his place as heir to the Fire Nation throne.

Zuko was banished by his father at a young age after speaking out of turn in a war council meeting and refusing to fight his father in an Agni Kai (a formal firebending duel), an act of defiance for which Ozai burned and scarred the left side of Zuko's face. Ozai declared that Zuko could only return home and restore his honor by capturing the Avatar, a quest that consumed Zuko's identity for years and was, in fact, designed by Ozai to be impossible, since the Avatar had vanished entirely for a century at that point.

Zuko is accompanied for most of his exile by his uncle, Iroh, a former Fire Nation general and once the rightful heir to the throne before Ozai, who gave up his claim to the crown after the death of his own son, Lu Ten, during the Siege of Ba Sing Se. Iroh serves as Zuko's primary moral guide throughout the series, gently steering him away from anger and toward self-understanding, despite Zuko's frequent resistance and hostility toward him.

After briefly capturing Aang and experiencing a period of restored status in the Fire Nation, Zuko ultimately rejects the path his father wants for him. He undergoes a major turning point when he learns the true, twisted history of the Fire Nation's war from the perspective of its victims, and ultimately defects to join Aang's group, becoming Aang's firebending teacher in Book 3. Zuko and Aang travel together to find the last two dragons, Ran and Shaw, where Zuko learns that true firebending should come from life and energy rather than rage, a pivotal moment in his redemption arc.

In the series finale, Zuko duels and defeats his sister Azula to claim the Fire Nation throne, becoming Fire Lord himself, while Aang spares Ozai's life by using energybending to permanently remove his firebending ability instead of killing him.

As Fire Lord, Zuko works to dismantle the legacy of the Hundred Year War, including searching for his missing mother, Ursa, and helping found Republic City alongside Aang as a neutral territory where people of all four nations could live together. Zuko remains an important background figure during the era of The Legend of Korra, by then an elderly statesman still respected as a symbol of the Fire Nation's reform.
