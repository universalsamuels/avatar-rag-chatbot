}}Iknik Blackstone Varrick, more commonly known simply as Varrick, was an eccentric and charismatic nonbending billionaire industrialist and businessman known for his ambition and flamboyant personality. Born in the Southern Water Tribe, he was a wealthy nobleman who traveled extensively aboard his yacht, openly indulging in his extravagant lifestyle, often at the expense of his former assistant and eventual wife, Zhu Li Moon.

A man who respected directness, he struck up a close friendship with the honest and candid earthbender Bolin, with whom he contributed in the development of movers. During the Water Tribe Civil War, he secretly orchestrated events intended to escalate the conflict between the Northern and Southern Water Tribes. Publicly, he claimed that Chief Unalaq's occupation of the South threatened the tribe's prosperity, including his own financial interests, while privately seeking to profit from the growing war. His scheme eventually extended to an attempt to draw the United Republic into the conflict, which ultimately resulted in his arrest.

Varrick later escaped imprisonment and eventually settled in Zaofu, where he became the head of the city's technology department. He subsequently accompanied Kuvira during her campaign to reunify the Earth Kingdom. However, after refusing to weaponize spirit energy for her regime, Varrick deserted the Earth Empire in 174 AG. He assisted Team Avatar in preventing Kuvira from conquering Republic City. Following Kuvira's defeat, Varrick married Zhu Li, and after she was elected President of the United Republic, he became the nation's First Gentleman.

History

Early life and career

Varrick was born to a poor seal-hunter. Growing up on a farm, he once had a pet ostrich horse named Mrs. Beaks, whom he loved but took for granted. At some point, he was taken away by the circus. As he grew up in the Southern Water Tribe, he managed to expand his wealth from a single canoe to a considerable business.

By the mid-150s AG, Varrick was somewhat well-known as a promising young investor in Republic City. The Southern Water Tribe inventor Wakkanai looked to Varrick's experience and funds after she helped to develop a new method for desalinating the water around Republic City, and Varrick was eager to invest in the new technology. However, when he learned that Wakkanai's partner Yinuo opposed his involvement, Varrick was equally eager to get rid of him.Avatar Legends: The Roleplaying Game. Wan Shi Tong's Adventure Guide, Version 1.0, 2022, p. 168.

When Wakkanai went looking for more investors, Varrick tampered with one of Yinuo's experiments, causing it to explode. Varrick seized the moment to present "evidence" that Yinuo's negligent experiments were endangering everyone. While Yinuo recognized that the evidence was false, Wakkanai had already begun to distrust her partner, and believed Varrick over him. Once Yinuo had been barred from further research by city regulators, Varrick helped Wakkanai negotiate a profitable arrangement with the city, giving her management of her water treatment centers in exchange for generational rights to the profits, allowing her daughter Kiaanii to be her heir rather than the rights reverting to the city. He was proud to have guaranteed that a fellow Southern Water Tribe citizen could keep her business safely within the family.

Varrick's business continued to grow into an empire, until he finally controlled the entire global shipping business and owned the world's largest corporation selling consumer goods. A consummate dealmaker, he is always looking for opportunities to expand his business empire.

Sometime after the Anti-bending Revolution, Varrick helped sponsor Raiko and his opponent in their respective candidacies for President of the United Republic of Nations, with Raiko winning the election.

171 AG to 174 AG

Role in the Water Tribe Civil War

In 171 AG, Varrick emerged as a major figure in Southern Water Tribe affairs when he met Asami Sato during the Glacier Spirits Festival and agreed to help save Future Industries from bankruptcy. During the same period, he quickly befriended Bolin, whose honesty and enthusiasm appealed to him, and he openly disrupted the formal atmosphere surrounding Chief Unalaq's visit by staging entertainment at a banquet. When tensions between the Northern and Southern Water Tribes escalated into violence, Varrick presented himself as an outspoken defender of the South, supplying resources and vehicles to Avatar Korra and her allies while warning that Unalaq's occupation threatened both Southern autonomy and his own commercial interests.

As the Water Tribe Civil War deepened, Varrick became more directly involved in resistance efforts against Unalaq. After being targeted for arrest, he hid with Zhu Li inside a stuffed platypus bear and later helped Korra's group evade the Northern blockade by providing both transport and a biplane. Once back in Republic City, however, he turned to more covert methods, promoting the Southern cause through propaganda movers starring Bolin as "Nuktuk" while also secretly arranging bombings and thefts meant to intensify anti-Northern sentiment. At the same time, he manipulated Asami's financial crisis to seize a controlling share in Future Industries and attempted to silence Mako once the firebender began suspecting him.

Varrick's schemes culminated in an attempt to kidnap President Raiko, blaming the incident on the North in order to force the United Republic of Nations into the war. The plan failed when Bolin exposed the conspiracy, leading to Varrick's arrest. Even in prison, however, he retained many of his luxuries, having anticipated one day needing a custom-built cell. When UnaVaatu attacked Republic City, the destruction of the prison gave him and Zhu Li an opportunity to escape. During the chaos, Varrick also became fascinated by the immense spiritual power unleashed during the battle, an experience that inspired his later interest in harnessing spirit energy.

Life in Zaofu and working for Kuvira

After fleeing Republic City, Varrick took refuge in Zaofu, where Suyin Beifong gave him a second chance by appointing him head of the city's technology division. There, he resumed his inventive work, proposed new transportation systems, and continued acting as an eccentric but useful industrialist. He also assisted Team Avatar indirectly when his familiarity with conspiracies helped reveal that Aiwei was secretly working for the Red Lotus. Not long afterward, he left Zaofu to join Kuvira's campaign to reunify the Earth Kingdom, where he served as an officer and engineer, modernized mecha tank designs, and eventually began experimenting with spirit vine energy for what he initially envisioned as a clean energy source.

By 174 AG, Varrick was fully embedded in Kuvira's military apparatus, but his enthusiasm began to falter when he realized Kuvira intended to weaponize his spirit vine research. Although he successfully extracted and redirected spirit vine energy in his experiments, the results were unstable and destructive, convincing him the project should be abandoned. Kuvira refused, threatening his life to force him to continue. This coercion, along with his growing awareness of his own conscience and the danger of the weapon, led Varrick to desert the Earth Empire alongside Bolin. His escape was complicated by Zhu Li's apparent betrayal, which left him emotionally shaken and convinced that he had finally lost the one person who had always remained by his side.

While fleeing the Earth Empire, Varrick and Bolin endured capture, pursuit, and near-death, eventually making their way back toward Republic City. Once they reached the capital, Varrick warned the world leaders that Kuvira was building a superweapon powered by spirit vines. Because of his expertise, he was reluctantly recruited by Raiko and Asami to help develop defenses against Kuvira's colossus. During the final defense of Republic City, he worked on the hummingbird mecha suits, proposed and failed with an electromagnetic pulse strategy, and finally reconciled with Zhu Li, proposing marriage just before the pair joined the assault on Kuvira's weapon.

Following Kuvira's defeat, Varrick married Zhu Li on Air Temple Island. In the aftermath of the invasion, he supported Zhu Li during the Evacuee Crisis and enthusiastically encouraged her to run for president against Raiko. He also used his flair for mover making to shape public opinion in her favor, documenting her heroism during the spirit portal crisis and helping elevate her campaign. After Zhu Li won the election, Varrick became the First Gentleman of the United Republic. As First Gentleman, Varrick frustrated Chief Beifong and the police by having several instances of disappearances, where he would always turn up unharmed in ridiculous circumstances a few days later. After disappearing for nearly a month when he thought up a new way to use moo-sow milk that would only work in the Spirit World, Beifong had to implement a "Varrick Rule" in missing persons cases, where Varrick had to be missing for at least a week before someone could even approach her about the issue.

At some point after Kuvira's trial, Varrick received a note from Yinuo, asking for a meeting to settle their old differences. Deeply ashamed of how he treated Yinuo all those years before, Varrick agreed to a meeting at the arena on the day of the Spring Pro-bending Tournament Qualifier's final match, hoping to set things right but also keep it quiet as not to unnecessarily expose his misdeeds to the public. As planned, he visited the arena on the morning before the tournament. While taking a break from the Presidential Box to "stretch his legs", Varrick went to the meeting, unaware that it was a trap. San Ho (the new identity of Yinuo) and his Terra Triad goons dragged Varrick away, intending to kill Varrick and ruin Wakkanai's business in the same blow by having the First Gentleman caught in an explosion at one of Wakkanai's water treatment facilities. When his security searched the area, they found no trace of him, and took the matter straight to President Moon. As she knew that the police would likely not take the matter seriously due to Varrick's track record and the "Varrick Rule", Zhu Li enlisted a small group of people at the arena and trusted them to save her husband, reaching out to Enuk, Changunak, Zhu Song, Slate, and Pianchir Jing.

Personality

Having grown up in relative poverty, Varrick learnt to be self-sufficient, to never take anything for granted, and to constantly strive for more. Once he had become one of the wealthiest people in the world, Varrick was used to having his every need catered to. Since no one was willing to disagree with him, he sometimes came to believe his own stories, such as when he thought he was levitating, a belief with which everyone went along. He was also a good judge of character, prepared for any issue well, and could quickly come up with a plan to avoid danger. However, at times he could also be impulsive, not thinking through all the consequences of his actions, like when he started a rebellion against Unalaq. This did not mean, however, that he was always incorrect about there being a need for action. Varrick loved being able to do what he pleased on his own terms, and as such, did not like to date clingy women, even going as far as building a yacht to escape from them.

Occasionally, Varrick could be openly rude and uncaring of others' feelings; this tendency was especially pronounced whenever he had new ideas, as his love for progress and technologies often led him to overlook moral concerns.Throughout The Legend of Korra.Avatar Legends: The Roleplaying Game. Wan Shi Tong's Adventure Guide, Version 1.0, 2022, p. 47. Varrick's eccentricities also masked his more dubious nature, as a sly and slick businessman who used underhanded means to get what he wants, even if it meant breaking the law. As such, he was not above being unfettered and charismatic, and was able to orchestrate large scale operations to buy out other companies just to increase his own wealth.

Despite this, Varrick did have a pronounced conscience, which he mistook as being "voices in his head". He generally strove to "build a better, brighter future" and considered hurting others to be beneath him and felt guilty upon realizing that his actions had brought harm to some of his allies. As a result, he helped Team Avatar, readily aiding them despite there being no monetary benefit, and was adamant about terminating his spirit vine project, wary about the destructive power it held if weaponized. He generally became more moralistic in course of his adventures alongside Team Avatar, ultimately coming to regret the various ruthless and illegal schemes of his early life.

Varrick was very dependent on Zhu Li, requiring her assistance for many different tasks, such as preparing his tea, scratching an irritable rash or to cater to him within prison. He frequently used the phrase, "Do the thing!" to prompt someone to take whatever action was necessary at any given time. In addition, he mostly relied on his men and associates to do his dirty work, as he rarely participated in any of his planned schemes in order to keep a low profile.

Following his marriage to Zhu Li, Varrick displayed a notably more mature personality, taking his wife's decision to run for President of the United Republic quite seriously, and putting aside his own needs to support her. He even put an entire film crew on the task of making Zhu Li look more impressive by capturing his wife's heroic actions during the Tokuga crisis, while simultaneously capturing Raiko's less flattering moments of self-preservation.

Abilities

Varrick was an innovative thinker and aggressive businessman, having been able to expand his company from one canoe to a global network. As an entrepreneur, Varrick was also keen on identifying and capitalizing on the needs of those around him. Similarly, he was able to convince others to work for his betterment, essentially having convinced the rebels to incite war against the North in order to benefit his business. Partly due to his company's technological advances, Varrick was capable of piloting ships and commandeering a yacht. Varrick was also physically fit, usually ending his brainstorming sessions by performing an elaborate flip off a bar he used to hang himself upside down.

As a genius technologist, Varrick was well-disciplined and knowledgeable in various fields of science, having spearheaded several technological advancements. His breakthroughs include applications of magnetism, such as a high-speed maglev train,DiMartino, Michael Dante; Konietzko, Bryan; Hedrick, Tim & Hamilton, Joshua (March 10, 2015). "The Coronation" commentary. Book Four: Balance Blu-ray. and the stabilization of spirit energy, though he later discovered that it was too dangerous for anyone to handle. He was also good at thinking on his feet, being able to create a makeshift electromagnetic pulse device with the aid of a metal table leg and an electric generator. At periods like these, he utilized his self-proclaimed "15 minutes of productivity" to engineer his creations. His tendency to constantly shift from one topic to another could make him difficult to work with, but he could be an capable and energizing teacher to those wishing to learn from him.

Varrick showed himself to be charismatic, which enabled him to talk his way out of many situations as well as entertain people by telling stories. He was also a master strategist, being able to plan false flag operations that his men or associates would execute at key moments.

Trivia

* Varrick is described as being "equal parts Howard Hughes and Richard Branson".
** His eccentric mannerisms and quick temper are also reminiscent of William Randolph Hearst, and his use of openly filmed footage of the civil war spliced with additional fake footage in his propaganda movers is similar to a practice Hearst used during World War I.
* Lauren Montgomery did the initial design for Varrick, with subsequent designs by Ki Hyun Ryu and Bryan Konietzko making him more aesthetically dashing while keeping the lankiness of Montgomery's concept.DiMartino, Michael Dante; Konietzko, Bryan; Dos Santos, Joaquim & Montgomery, Lauren (July 1, 2014). "The Guide" commentary. Book Two: Spirits Blu-ray.
* Varrick was referred to as a "captain of industry". Although the author is not an official part of the staff of The Legend of Korra, she acquired this info directly from SDCC 2012.
* Varrick was a self-proclaimed "biz-bender".
* The creators have joked that Varrick is able to run his various businesses by never stopping for bathroom breaks.DiMartino, Michael Dante; Konietzko, Bryan & Dos Santos, Joaquim (July 1, 2014). "Civil Wars, Part 1" commentary. Book Two: Spirits Blu-ray.
* Varrick invented the moving picture, which he calls "movers", and his first "mover" is a reference to Sallie Gardner at a Gallop, one of the first photographic experiments which led to the development of motion pictures.
* Varrick had a habit of using his name as a prefix in almost every product he advertised, such as Varri-dye and Varri-cakes.
* Varrick named a measure of power after himself and a measure of weight after his assistant, Zhu Li.
* Despite having designed the modernized mecha suits, he was not proficient in piloting them.
* Varrick is the third character in The Legend of Korra, after Tarrlok and Ghazan, to attempt suicide, though he is the only one to have survived.
* Varrick was shown to be knowledgeable of the Chinese hand system when he used the gesture for "six" when saying "six wolfbats".
* Varrick and Zhu Li are the fourth couple in the whole franchise to be seen getting married, after Roku and Ta Min, Ozai and Ursa, and Ikem and Ursa.
