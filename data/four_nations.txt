The Four Nations

The world of Avatar is divided into four nations, each historically associated with one of the four bending elements, though not every citizen of a nation is necessarily a bender.

The Water Tribes are split primarily into the Southern Water Tribe and Northern Water Tribe, located at the poles of the world. The Southern Water Tribe, home to Katara and Sokka, was a small and struggling community by the time of the original series, having been repeatedly raided by the Fire Nation, which specifically targeted and captured Southern waterbenders to prevent resistance. The Northern Water Tribe, by contrast, remained large, fortified, and traditionally conservative, with strict gender roles initially barring women like Katara from learning combat waterbending, only allowing them to train as healers, a restriction Katara directly challenged and overcame. There is also a Foggy Swamp Tribe, an offshoot Water Tribe community living in a swamp in the Earth Kingdom, and by Korra's era, a city called Republic City sits near former Earth Kingdom territory as a multicultural hub.

The Earth Kingdom is the largest of the four nations both in population and territory, ruled from the capital city of Ba Sing Se, governed during the original series by the reclusive and largely powerless Earth King Kuei, who was kept ignorant of the war by the secretive and authoritarian Dai Li, the kingdom's secret police force. Ba Sing Se is famously divided into rigid concentric rings (Lower, Middle, and Upper) reflecting class stratification within the city. The Earth Kingdom encompasses numerous smaller cities and towns seen throughout the series, including Omashu, ruled by King Bumi, an old friend of Aang's. By the time of The Legend of Korra, the Earth Kingdom's central authority has collapsed following the death of the Earth Queen, eventually leading to the rise of Kuvira and the kingdom's controversial reorganization into the Earth Empire and later the Earth Republic.

The Fire Nation is an industrially and militarily advanced island nation that initiated the Hundred Year War under Fire Lord Sozin, who used the appearance of Sozin's Comet (which dramatically empowers firebenders) to launch the genocidal extermination of the Air Nomads and begin a century of imperial conquest across the other nations. The Fire Nation is ruled by a line of Fire Lords; Sozin was succeeded by Azulon, then by Ozai, the primary antagonist of the original series, whose children are Zuko and Azula. After Ozai's defeat, Zuko becomes Fire Lord and works to repair the Fire Nation's relationship with the other nations, including establishing colonies as independent territory and helping found Republic City.

The Air Nomads were a nomadic, spiritually-focused people organized around four Air Temples, located at the cardinal compass points of the world: the Southern Air Temple, Northern Air Temple, Eastern Air Temple, and Western Air Temple. Air Nomad society was organized around monasteries with monks and nuns living relatively simple lives focused on meditation, spiritual growth, and non-attachment, including a rule against forming exclusive romantic attachments, which created tension for Aang regarding his feelings for Katara. The Air Nomads were entirely wiped out by the Fire Nation a century before the start of the original series, leaving Aang as the sole survivor until the airbending re-emergence event during Korra's era, after which a new Air Nation was founded under Tenzin's guidance, eventually relocating to the Southern Air Temple.
