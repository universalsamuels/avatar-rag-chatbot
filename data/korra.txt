Korra

Korra is the Avatar succeeding Aang and the protagonist of The Legend of Korra. Unlike Aang, who struggled with the burden of his identity, Korra was identified as the Avatar at a very young age and grew up knowing and embracing her role. Born into the Southern Water Tribe, Korra was a natural waterbender, earthbender, and firebender from a young age, mastering three of the four elements before her airbending, which she struggled with for years.

Korra trained airbending intensively only after meeting Tenzin, Aang and Katara's son, who became her primary mentor and a recurring figure throughout the series. Korra's personality is markedly different from Aang's: she is headstrong, physically confident, and prone to using force first, which often put her at odds with the more patient, diplomatic approach embodied by Aang and his teachings.

Over the course of The Legend of Korra, she faces several major antagonists representing different ideological threats to the world: Amon and the Equalists, who sought to strip all benders of their bending abilities in the name of equality for non-benders; Unalaq and the Dark Avatar State crisis involving spirits merging with the physical world; the Earth Queen's authoritarian regime and the rise of Kuvira, a former protégé of Suyin Beifong who attempted to forcibly reunify the fractured Earth Kingdom under her own military rule; and Zaheer, the leader of the Red Lotus, an anarchist group that believed the Avatar cycle itself should be ended to bring about a world without centralized authority.

Korra's battle with Zaheer left her poisoned with mercury-laced metal and physically and psychologically traumatized, leading to a multi-year period where she struggled to recover both physically and from post-traumatic stress, a storyline notable for its realistic portrayal of trauma recovery in a children's animated series.

Korra is openly bisexual, and the series finale confirms a romantic relationship between Korra and Asami Sato, the wealthy industrialist and engineer who became one of Korra's closest friends and allies. This was confirmed explicitly by the creators after the show's conclusion, making Korra one of the first lead protagonists in a Nickelodeon animated series confirmed to be in a same-sex relationship.

Korra's animal guide is Naga, a polar bear dog. Throughout the series, Korra grapples with what it means to be the Avatar in a rapidly modernizing world during the technological boom led by Republic City, balancing ancient spiritual responsibilities against industrialization, political instability, and the changing relationship between benders and non-benders.
