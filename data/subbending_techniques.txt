Subbending Techniques and Specialized Bending

Subbending refers to specialized techniques that branch off from the four main bending arts. These are advanced, rare, or unique abilities that only certain benders can perform.

Bloodbending

Bloodbending is a rare and forbidden technique within waterbending that allows a waterbender to control the water inside a living creature's body, effectively controlling their movements like a puppet. It was invented by Hama, a Southern Water Tribe waterbender who was captured and imprisoned by the Fire Nation. While imprisoned, she discovered she could draw moisture from the air and eventually realized she could control the water inside living creatures.

Bloodbending was originally only possible during a full moon, when a waterbender's power is at its peak. However, exceptionally powerful waterbenders like Katara and Yakone could perform bloodbending without the full moon. Yakone, a notorious Republic City crime lord, was so powerful he could bloodbend an entire room of people simultaneously without the full moon. His sons Noatak (Amon) and Tarrlok also inherited this ability.

Bloodbending is considered the darkest and most forbidden waterbending technique. After the events of the original series, the Northern and Southern Water Tribes outlawed bloodbending entirely, making it illegal to practice or teach.

Lightning Generation and Redirection

Lightning generation is an advanced firebending technique that involves separating the positive and negative energies within the body and releasing them in a focused bolt of electricity. It requires complete inner peace and the absence of emotion, since conflicting emotions cause the energies to become unstable and dangerous to the bender themselves.

In Avatar: The Last Airbender, only highly skilled firebenders can generate lightning, including Ozai, Azula, and eventually Zuko. Azula was particularly gifted at lightning generation, able to produce it quickly and precisely.

Lightning redirection is a separate but related technique invented by Iroh, who studied the waterbending principle of redirecting an opponent's force rather than meeting it head on. By guiding lightning through a specific path in the body — in one arm, through the stomach, and out the other arm — a bender can safely redirect a lightning strike away from themselves. Zuko learned this technique from Iroh and used it during his Agni Kai against Azula to save Katara, though he took the lightning strike directly into his own chest instead of redirecting it fully, nearly killing him.

Combustion Bending

Combustion bending is an extremely rare and unique firebending technique in which a bender can project a focused beam of energy from a point on their forehead (the third eye chakra) that causes objects to explode on contact. Unlike regular firebending which uses the breath and body, combustion bending bypasses normal firebending mechanics entirely.

In Avatar: The Last Airbender, only one combustion bender is shown — a silent assassin hired by Zuko to kill Aang, nicknamed Combustion Man (real name unknown, later revealed in supplementary material to be Sparky Sparky Boom Man). He has a mechanical right arm and a tattoo on his forehead at the point where the energy is focused.

In The Legend of Korra, another combustion bender named P'Li serves as a member of the Red Lotus. She is capable of projecting curved combustion beams. Both combustion benders can be stopped by disrupting the flow of energy at the forehead point — Combustion Man is defeated when Sokka's boomerang strikes his tattoo, and P'Li is defeated when Ming-Hua and Ghazan's attack causes her combustion to detonate at close range.

Metalbending

Metalbending is a specialized earthbending technique invented by Toph Beifong. Toph discovered that processed metal still contains trace amounts of earth in its impure form, and by sensing and controlling those impurities, an earthbender can manipulate metal just as they would bend rock or soil.

Metalbending was previously thought impossible because metal is too refined and processed to be earthbent. Toph's extraordinary seismic sense, developed as a blind earthbender who perceived the world through vibrations in the ground, allowed her to detect the minute earth particles within metal that others could not sense.

After Toph's discovery, metalbending became a distinct and respected subset of earthbending. By the time of The Legend of Korra, metalbending is widespread enough that Toph founded the Metalbending Police Force in Republic City, which uses metal cables and armor for law enforcement. Suyin Beifong, Toph's daughter, established Zaofu, an entire city built from and governed by metalbenders.

Lavabending

Lavabending is an extremely rare and powerful subset of earthbending that allows a bender to melt rock into lava and control its flow. Unlike regular earthbending which manipulates solid rock, lavabending requires sensing the molecular structure of earth and exciting it to a molten state.

Only a handful of benders in recorded history have demonstrated lavabending ability. Ghazan, a member of the Red Lotus in The Legend of Korra, is a powerful lavabender who uses it as his primary combat technique. Bolin, initially thought to be a standard earthbender, discovers his lavabending ability during a critical moment against Unalaq, making him one of the few non-Avatar lavabenders known. The Avatar can also lavabend while in the Avatar State.

Sandbending

Sandbending is a regional earthbending style practiced by earthbenders in desert regions, particularly in the Si Wong Desert. Because sand is too fine and loose to be manipulated using traditional earthbending techniques, sandbenders developed unique methods to control it. Sandbenders in the Si Wong Desert use large sand-sailers, vehicles propelled by manipulating sand with earthbending.

Seismic Sense

Seismic sense is not a bending technique per se but a specialized skill developed by Toph Beifong and the badgermoles. It involves detecting vibrations in the ground to perceive the surrounding environment without using sight. Toph, being blind, developed this ability to an extraordinary degree, effectively using the earth as a substitute for vision. Her seismic sense allows her to detect heartbeats, sense footsteps, identify people by their gait, and perceive the layout of her surroundings through the ground. It does not work when she is standing on sand, in the air, or on wooden surfaces, which are the main limitations of the technique.

Energybending

Energybending is the oldest and rarest form of bending, predating the four elemental arts. It involves bending the life energy within a person rather than an external element. The original practitioners of energybending were the Lion Turtles, massive ancient creatures who granted humans the ability to bend the four elements by energybending.

Energybending can be used to grant or permanently remove a person's bending ability. It requires complete spiritual purity in the practitioner — any darkness in the bender's spirit will be overpowered by the darkness in the person they are bending, destroying the practitioner. Aang learned energybending from the last Lion Turtle and used it to permanently remove Ozai's firebending rather than killing him. In The Legend of Korra, Amon's chi-blocking technique mimics the effect of energybending but is actually a bloodbending technique targeting specific chi pathways.
