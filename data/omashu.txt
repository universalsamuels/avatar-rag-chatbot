}}Omashu is the second largest city in the Earth Kingdom and the capital of one of its states. Surpassed in size and importance only by Ba Sing Se, Omashu was one of the last great strongholds of the Earth Kingdom during the Hundred Year War, and it continued to supply the Earth Kingdom Army with troops and supplies until it was taken by Fire Nation forces in the closing months of the great war. The gates of this bustling metropolis are guarded by an army of earthbenders. This city is also well known for its famous delivery system powered by a combination of both earthbending and gravity. Due to its size, importance, and unique innovations, Omashu has always competed with Ba Sing Se for prominence in the Earth Kingdom.Avatar Legends: The Roleplaying Game. Core Book, Version 1.0, 2022, p. 23.

The tall mountain peak on which Omashu was founded is located in the Kolau Mountain Range in the southwestern Earth Kingdom. It rises high out of a deep chasm and was created by earthbenders several centuries prior to King Bumi's reign. Being built on this mountain provides the city with one of its best defenses; the only way to reach the gates of Omashu is across a long narrow bridge of stone that spans the sheer gorge.

Previously ruled by Bumi, Omashu was conquered by the Fire Nation during the War and renamed New Ozai. However, during the Day of Black Sun, the powerless firebenders were expelled from the city, and it was once again reclaimed by the Earth Kingdom.

History

Founding

 that tells the story of Oma and Shu.]]

An ancient legend states that a man named Shu and a woman named Oma, each from one of two warring towns, met atop a mountain that divided their peoples and fell deeply in love. Although their relationship was forbidden and it was dangerous for them to continue meeting, the couple found a way to continue seeing each other in secrecy. By observing the badgermoles, the couple learned how to earthbend. Using their new powers, they created a great labyrinth which only they could navigate. This allowed them to meet secretly, while anyone else who tried to follow them would become lost forever in the ever-shifting tunnels.

The couple's romance ended in tragedy when Shu was slain in the war between their two villages. Oma, stricken with grief and sorrow, performed a terrifying display of earthbending power that could have easily destroyed both villages. Instead, she declared the conflict over and demanded that the two villages live in peace. The warring villages joined together and built a new city to honor the couple's love. This city eventually grew into Omashu, the name being derived from the combination of their two names. The short epitaph found on the lovers' tombs deep within the cave reads only "Love is brightest in the dark".

Early prosperity

Early in its history, Omashu implemented charitable policies that successfully reduced the divide between the rich and poor in the city. Omashu flourished by the time of Avatar Yangchen, becoming a major population and trading center. Even after the Earth Kingdom ports were closed after the Platinum Affair, there was a huge amount of money to be made on the export of Omashu silk, which partially led to the creation of shang cities open to international trade.

By Avatar Kuruk's time, free trade had resumed, and Omashu continued to profit. Several harbors supplied the city with goods, and one of Omashu's most important trading partners during this time was Shimsom Big Island. Its riches also attracted criminals, and the Flying Opera Company set up a secret base in the city.

Following the death of Kuruk in 312 BG, King Buro of Omashu took part in the search for the next Avatar, but he failed like all before him. In the subsequent years, the ruling elite of Omashu became close allies of the powerful Earth Sage Jianzhu and helped him to become the country's de facto regent. At some point between 298 and 296 BG, the princess of Omashu developed a romantic interest in Jianzhu's pupil, the false Avatar Yun, and influenced her city to sign an important trade deal to Jianzhu's benefit.

During the conquests of Chin the Conqueror, almost the entirety of the Earth Kingdom, Omashu being no exception, fell into the hands of this tyrannical warlord. The city remained under Chin's rule until his death following his confrontation with Avatar Kyoshi.

Competition with Ba Sing Se

In her later life, Kyoshi supported the family of Guo Xun taking the throne with the sacred duty to protect Omashu.Avatar Legends: The Roleplaying Game. Core Book, Version 1.0, 2022, p. 54. The city-state prospered tremendously as international trade rapidly expanded, and Omashu was able to project its political power over all western states of the Earth Kingdom under the reign of Queen Guo Xun. It enjoyed a considerable trade surplus with the Fire Nation, and started to undercut Fire Lord Taiso's own merchants. Nevertheless, Taiso's government supported a policy of free trade, and sought good relations with Omashu in order to maintain an effective buffer state between the Fire Nation and Ba Sing Se and weaken the Earth Kingdom as a whole on the world stage.

In 63 BG, Guo Xun's Minister of Peace, Huaji, conspired with Ba Sing Se's Minister of Peace, Pushi, an agent of Earth King Jialun, to cause a catastrophe in Omashu in order for both Huaji and Jialun to end Guo Xun's successful reign as queen. After the master poisoner known as Atka successfully rid the city of its scorpion rats, Huaji asked her to create death for him with the spiritually potent Lambak stone he had acquired. Their plot was discovered by Ta Min, a Fire Nation emissary in the city who was acting as Prince Sozin's spy. Even though Sozin was informed of the plot, he chose to do nothing, as he believed it would be in the Fire Nation's interest to have a wealthy rival ruined and to expand the power of a weak and unintelligent King Jialun.

Ta Min tried to investigate the Lambak stone herself at Huaji's villa, but was captured and imprisoned for allegedly trying to assassinate the minister. Ta Min managed to escape her cell at the same time Sozin's head bodyguard, Kozaru, came to break her out of jail. However, believing Kozaru and Sozin had ill intentions, Ta Min returned to the villa for a sample of the Lambak stone, and fled the city for the Northern Water Tribe on Kozaru's eel hound. Atka was unable to return to Omashu due to Avatar Roku following her, and chose to use her poison on Agna Qel'a instead, where she had more grievances. Avatar Roku later wrote a message to Guo Xun to clear Ta Min's name and warn her about Huaji. Ta Min, meanwhile, offered to personally deliver the message and warn her about Jialun and his schemes in person, intending to serve as an emissary in the name of peace instead of working as Sozin's spy.

After learning of Jialun's plot, Guo Xun's rivalry with the Earth King deeply intensified. She strongly opposed his actions to purge the Earth Sages, and Omashu and Ba Sing Se began to compete with each other more than ever. Omashu's queen did all she could to undermine the Earth King's power, short of starting an all-out war. Both monarchs had spies in each other's courts, and both throne rooms were vipers' nests. When the Omashu delivery system started to malfunction, the queen suspected further sabotage from one of the Earth King's agents.Avatar Legends: The Roleplaying Game. Core Book, Version 1.0, 2022, p. 55.

During their early childhood, Bumi, who would later become King of Omashu, and Avatar Aang used the Omashu delivery system as a slide.

Hundred Year War

Early conflict and growing isolation

Following the commencement of the Hundred Year War in 0 AG, Omashu became one of the most important centers of resistance against the advancing Fire Nation military. Omashu's main industry was producing weapons and supplies for the war effort; civilians had developed a complex and efficient mail system used to transport goods throughout the city that employed earthbending and gravity using extensive ramps and chutes. The war saw considerable restrictions on bringing contraband or poor-quality goods into the city, and citizens were quick to distance themselves from anything that could be considered to have any associations with the Fire Nation.

After Bumi rose to power, he further strengthened Omashu's position as a supplier to Earth Kingdom military units and militias across the western continent. He also invited inventors to his great city to design new weapons. Yet as the Fire Army advanced, Omashu became increasingly isolated and encircled.Avatar Legends: The Roleplaying Game. Core Book, Version 1.0, 2022, p. 62. Though the great city initially endured, its ability to support the rest of the Earth Kingdom became limited. This left Omashu largely unable to impact the wider war by the 90s AG.Avatar Legends: The Roleplaying Game. Wan Shi Tong's Adventure Guide, Version 1.0, 2022, p. 124. Regardless, Fire Lord Ozai made the conquest of Omashu one of his main priorities after ascending his throne in 95 AG.

In late 99 AG, Aang and his new comrades Katara and Sokka visited Omashu. Bumi involved the group in a series of tests, eventually revealing his identity to the Avatar's great joy. Around this time, a team of adventurers also helped some local citizens, including by dealing with a group of criminals.

In early 100 AG, Omashu was seized by Fire Nation forces after the Fire Nation developed long, metal, extending bridges in order to cross the gorge and directly assault the city. King Bumi, always pragmatic and enigmatic, surrendered the city to the Fire Nation forces without a fight. The entire city underwent massive reconstruction to better suit the needs of the Fire Nation. The construction of a massive statue of Fire Lord Ozai at the city's peak began, and the palace was completely leveled and replaced with the metal palace architecture favored by the Fire Nation. This may have been in part due to the fact that the old palace was designed specifically for earthbenders and as such most rooms could only be accessed through the use of earthbending. This would make entrance and exit into some of these rooms impossible for non-earthbenders. Many of the ordinary buildings were commandeered by the Fire Nation and turned into factories with chimneys protruding the top.

Ukano became the Governor of New Ozai, replacing the deposed King Bumi, who was taken into custody after surrendering the city. However, many citizens and soldiers formed a rebel group, the "Omashu Resistance", though quickly started to struggle with survival as they hid in the city's sewer system. The city was renamed New Ozai in honor of Fire Lord Ozai by his daughter, Princess Azula, in spring 100 AG. Azula's team also battled Team Avatar as the latter sought a way to rescue Bumi; ultimately, the king remained imprisoned, but Avatar Aang helped the Omashu Resistance to flee the city. Attempting to reinforce Fire Nation control in the city after Team Avatar's escape, Azula's harsh actions eventually provoked an unsuccessful uprising among local citizens.

 Bumi liberated Omashu on the Day of Black Sun.]]

During the Day of Black Sun in summer 100 AG, the imprisoned Bumi saw that the time to liberate the city and release himself from imprisonment had come. Following that, he proceeded to defeat the powerless firebenders and single-handedly reclaim his city from the Fire Nation; he even went as far as defiling and destroying the recently completed statue of Ozai, destroying the New Ozai palace, while laughing maniacally and eating jennamite.

Anarchy in the Earth Kingdom

Following Earth Queen Hou-Ting's death and the fragmentation of the Earth Kingdom, Omashu and its state continued to resist any attempts by Kuvira to integrate it into her empire for three years. Shortly after the Earth Kingdom's transformation into the Earth Empire, however, Omashu yielded to her regime and remained under her control until she was arrested following her failed attempt to annex the United Republic of Nations.

Government

 holds audiences in their throne room.]]

The city of Omashu is ruled by a monarch. The monarchs traditionally exercise great control over their subjects, while also having the "sacred duty" to protect the city and its citizens. Aided by several ministers, the monarchs exert power over the city's law enforcement, military, foreign affairs, and economic policies. In addition, the title of "princess of Omashu" was in use by the 3rd century BG.

During the time in which Omashu was occupied by the Fire Nation, a governor was appointed by Fire Lord Ozai himself. This governor exercised both civilian and military control in the name of the Fire Nation central government, as demonstrated in his blunt ordering of all the citizens to be evacuated. He was answerable to the Fire Lord or the Royal Family.

Layout and description

 run throughout the city.]]

The city of Omashu sits within the Kolau Mountain Range, surrounded on all sides by steep canyons and an enormous gorge. The city's three gates are composed of massive blocks of stone, each five feet thick and over thirty feet tall.The Lost Scrolls: Earth, page 111 of The Lost Scrolls Collection. As a result, the gates can only be opened by skilled earthbenders who guard its only entrance. The city itself is built on a number of hills. The palace sits on the tallest hill, which is centered behind some other hills. The city has thousands of houses for ordinary citizens and nobles as well as hundreds of shops, restaurants and pottery workshops. Most types of businesses in Ba Sing Se would be present in Omashu as well. The city also has a complex mail system, in which packages are transported using earthbending and gravity. Buildings in Omashu are generally built from stone, unlike most buildings in the Earth Kingdom, and have green tiled roofs.

Though Omashu's government implemented successful policies to reduce the negative effects of income gaps among its citizenry, there remained a clearly observable divide between the homes of poor and rich citizens. The lower classes generally resided in Omashu's lower levels, where houses tended to fairly small. The homes of the upper classes were concentrated in the upper levels, and some villas had individual furnishings which were worth more than an entire low-level house. By the 1st century BG, the wealthiest houses in Omashu had in-built heated floors, a marvel of engineering at the time.

During the Fire Nation occupation of the city, the stone was in the process of being paved over and replaced by steel; scaffoldings were a common sight. The city produced pollution as a result of the Fire Nation's technology, which produced a large number of fumes. A massive statue of Fire Lord Ozai was constructed in the center of the city. Soldiers also patrolled the city, enforcing the new laws on the citizens. A banner depicting the Fire Nation emblem hung over the gateway leading to the city's entrance. In addition, the original earthen gate was replaced by a metal one, enabling non-earthbenders to access the city.

Locations

Royal Palace

The Omashu Royal Palace is the traditional home to the city's monarch prior and is located at the top of the Omashu's central mountain peak. It was a palace consisting entirely of earthbending-operated doors and would be impossible for anyone, with the exception of an earthbender, to navigate it.

Omashu sewer system

The Omashu sewer system is a series of interconnected tunnels underneath the city of Omashu, used to transport water and sewage out of the city. The sewage system is a very dark and dank place full of all sorts of filth and unusual critters including the leech-a-pillar and purple pentapus. The sewer system ends at the bottom of the gorge and is one of the only ways to sneak in and out of Omashu. Aang knew about these secret passages from past experiences playing there with Bumi. Aang navigated the system accompanied by Katara and Sokka when they wanted to enter the city during its occupation by the Fire Nation.

Markets

Omashu's markets consisted of various shops, restaurants, and stands. A restaurant called Double Dumpling was known for its famous spicy dish, the Dragon Dumplings, which was very popular among the city's residents and got quickly sold out. Another known location was Jade's General Goods, a humble shop selling a variety of goods, including egg custard tart. In addition to money, purchases could be made using special Omashu vouchers, which were said to be accepted at any officially recognized business in the city. There was also an animal shelter next to the markets.

After the Fire Nation seized control of the city, the former royal palace was replaced with a new one constructed to house the governor and his family. It was situated at a lower elevation and exhibited a grand display of traditional Fire Nation architectural features. The building was three stories tall and had a tiered structure mimicking that of a temple, the uppermost tier being the smallest. Shingles covered the roof, which was red in coloration and lined by a yellow trimming along the edges. Off to the palace's sides were mail delivery lines, and the entire structure was elevated above the ground.

The palace featured many spacious balconies, which were made from yellow stone and had numerous golden ornaments that lined the railing. Ash-colored pillars supported the balcony on the second floor, and behind these pillars was a similarly toned wall bearing the building's main entrance. The second story also had a dressing room, where the palace's many cushions and baby toys were kept, though often scattered along the floor. At least two stuffed animals were stored in the room, one being a teddy bear. At the room's center lay a table where a bowl of assorted fruits would be left for the palace's residents to eat, and beside it was a brown cabinet.

Mail system

 and Aang played on the mail system.]]

The Omashu mail system is a linking slide that continues for miles in the city. Earthbenders move the packages around to a different slide which can travel to any place in the city. Aang and Bumi used it as a "super slide" just before the start of the War. One hundred years later, Aang used the system as a slide with Katara and Sokka, although this time, he was a bit wild, using airbending to increase their speed. Aang utilized the chutes again when trying to recover the captured King Bumi from Azula.
