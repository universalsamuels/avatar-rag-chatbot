Major Antagonists of The Legend of Korra

Amon, real name Noatak, is the leader of the Equalists, a movement of non-benders in Republic City who believe benders use their abilities to oppress and dominate non-bending citizens. Amon possesses the rare ability to permanently remove a person's bending through a touch-based technique later revealed to be a form of bloodbending applied to block a person's chi pathways, a skill he learned from his father, Yakone, a notorious bloodbending criminal. Amon is eventually revealed to be Tarrlok's brother, both sons of Yakone, and his movement collapses after his deception and true identity are exposed.

Unalaq is Korra's uncle and the chief of the Northern Water Tribe, who initially presents himself as a spiritual guide trying to reconnect Korra with the spirit world after she neglects this aspect of her Avatar training. Unalaq is later revealed to be working to merge the human and spirit worlds by force and free Vaatu, an ancient malevolent spirit of darkness and chaos who was sealed away ten thousand years earlier by Wan, the first Avatar. Unalaq becomes a Dark Avatar by fusing with Vaatu, and Korra defeats him by entering the Avatar State and battling him as Raava, the spirit of light fused with the Avatar line, restoring balance.

Zaheer is the leader of the Red Lotus, a splinter faction of the Order of the White Lotus that believes true freedom requires the dismantling of all forms of centralized government, including the nations and the Avatar's role in maintaining the political order. Zaheer is a master airbender despite not being born an Air Nomad, having gained the ability to fly without a glider through extreme spiritual enlightenment after achieving a selfless state of mind. Zaheer and the Red Lotus orchestrate the kidnapping and poisoning of Korra with mercury-laced metal, an event that leaves her with lasting physical and psychological trauma, including severe PTSD, for an extended period afterward.

Kuvira is a former protégé of Suyin Beifong of Zaofu who rises to power amid the political chaos following the death of the Earth Queen. Kuvira positions herself as a stabilizing military force, reunifying the fractured Earth Kingdom by force under the banner of the "Earth Empire," eventually revealing increasingly authoritarian and expansionist ambitions, including the development of a massive mecha weapon powered by spirit vine energy. Kuvira's storyline is notably framed with sympathy as well as criticism, exploring how legitimate desires for order and stability can curdle into authoritarianism. She is ultimately defeated by Korra not through a final violent confrontation but through Korra's empathy and willingness to understand Kuvira's fear and motivations.
