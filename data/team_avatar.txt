Team Avatar and Key Supporting Characters

Katara is a waterbender from the Southern Water Tribe and one of Aang's closest companions, eventually becoming his wife. Katara is one of the last waterbenders of the Southern Water Tribe and becomes a master waterbender over the course of the series, eventually training under Master Pakku at the Northern Water Tribe after challenging the tribe's restriction against women learning combat waterbending. She is also a skilled healer and one of the only known practitioners of bloodbending, though she views the technique with horror after being forced to learn it from the criminal Hama. Katara is known for her strong moral compass, maternal protectiveness over the group, and her quick temper when it comes to injustice.

Sokka is Katara's older brother from the Southern Water Tribe, initially a non-bender who relies on strategy, technology, and his boomerang and later a sword gifted to him by the swordmaster Piandao. Sokka provides much of the series' comic relief but also grows into a skilled tactician and leader, playing a key role in planning major battles such as the invasion during the Day of Black Sun. He has a romantic relationship with Suki, the leader of the Kyoshi Warriors, an all-female group of non-bending warriors trained in a fighting style based on Avatar Kyoshi, an earlier Avatar.

Toph Beifong is a blind earthbending prodigy from a wealthy Earth Kingdom family who joins Team Avatar partway through the series. Toph's blindness led her to develop "seismic sense," perceiving her surroundings through vibrations in the earth, a skill she later credits to observing badgermoles. She invents metalbending, a previously unknown subset of earthbending, by recognizing that metal retains impurities of the earth it was extracted from. After the war, Toph founds the first metalbending police force in Republic City and later, in The Legend of Korra era, runs a swamp-based metalbending academy; her daughters, Lin Beifong and Suyin Beifong, become major characters in Korra's story as, respectively, the chief of the Republic City police and the matriarch of Zaofu, a hidden city of metalbenders.

Azula is Zuko's younger sister, a firebending prodigy considered by their father, Ozai, to be the superior child and rightful heir. Azula is calculating, ruthless, and one of the few characters shown capable of generating lightning at a young age. She is accompanied by her friends Mai and Ty Lee, who along with Azula make up a secondary antagonist trio for much of the series. Azula's mental state deteriorates significantly by the series finale, culminating in her breakdown during her Agni Kai with Zuko.

Iroh, Zuko's uncle, is a former Fire Nation general famous for his unsuccessful but legendary siege of Ba Sing Se, after which he largely renounced violence and conquest following the death of his son. Iroh is a firebending master, one of the only known users of lightning redirection, and is associated with the Order of the White Lotus, a secret society of masters from all four nations dedicated to wisdom and balance rather than national loyalty.

Tenzin is Aang and Katara's son, an airbending master and one of the few connections to Air Nomad heritage during the time of The Legend of Korra. Tenzin serves as Korra's primary airbending teacher and a political and spiritual leader in Republic City, often representing a more traditional, measured counterpoint to Korra's impulsiveness. He has three children seen prominently in the series: Jinora, Ikki, and Meelo.

Asami Sato is the daughter of Hiroshi Sato, a wealthy Republic City industrialist who secretly leads the Equalist movement against benders. Asami is a non-bender, but a highly skilled engineer, inventor, and combatant who becomes one of Korra's closest friends and eventually her romantic partner, as confirmed in the series finale of The Legend of Korra.
